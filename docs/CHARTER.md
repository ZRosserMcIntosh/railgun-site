# Railgun Charter

**Version 1.0 — Ratified January 2026**

---

## Preamble

Railgun is civic infrastructure. It exists to lower the cost of truth, protect the ability to communicate without permission, and enable peaceful coordination at scale. It is not a weapon. It is a road, a printing press, a public utility for the information age.

We build infrastructure that makes violence unnecessary by making truth accessible, lies expensive, and peaceful self-correction possible.

---

## The Ten Principles

### 1. Non-Violence Is Non-Negotiable
Railgun never advocates, facilitates, or enables violence, coercion, or physical harm. The only legitimate use of force is immediate self-defense of persons. The system is designed to *reduce* the conditions that lead to violence by making peaceful alternatives viable.

### 2. Infrastructure, Not Agitation
We build roads, not barricades. Printing presses, not weapons. Our purpose is to provide neutral, durable infrastructure that any community can use for lawful purposes. We do not organize movements, target individuals, or frame any party as an enemy.

### 3. Truth Without Permission
Every person has the right to communicate, to publish evidence, and to coordinate with others—without requiring approval from any central authority. Railgun exists to make that right technically enforceable, not merely aspirational.

### 4. Sovereignty At The Edge
Users control their own keys, their own data, and their own relationships. Railgun operators cannot read messages, cannot identify users beyond minimal routing metadata, and cannot comply with demands to produce data they do not possess.

### 5. The Protocol Survives The Operator
The core protocol must remain functional even if Railgun (the organization) is compromised, acquired, bankrupted, or legally compelled to shut down. No single point of failure. No kill switch. The infrastructure belongs to everyone who uses it.

### 6. Layered Separation Protects Legitimacy
The Sovereign Core (protocol) is neutral and non-commercial. The Services Layer (enterprise, compliance, performance) is where business happens. These layers are architecturally separate so that commercial pressures cannot corrupt the core, and legal pressures on the business layer cannot disable the protocol.

### 7. Safe Defaults, Deliberate Power
Default settings must be safe, boring, and appropriate for ordinary communication. Features that carry elevated risk (anonymity tools, evidence publishing, coordination at scale) require explicit user intent and informed consent. Power is available but never automatic.

### 8. Minimal Retention, Maximum Deletion
We retain the minimum data necessary for routing and never longer than required. Users can delete their data. When data must exist for legal compliance, it is isolated in the Services Layer—never in the Sovereign Core.

### 9. Legitimacy Through Transparency
Railgun publishes its protocols, audits its code, discloses its funding, and operates in the open. We do not rely on obscurity. Legitimacy is earned through accountability, not claimed through rhetoric.

### 10. No Enemies, Only Problems
We do not personalize adversaries. We do not name targets. We do not celebrate the misfortune of others. We describe problems (censorship, surveillance, coercion) and build solutions. The goal is a world where everyone—including those who currently oppose us—can communicate safely.

## Operational Commitments

- **Annual Ethics Audit**: Independent review of product decisions against this Charter.
- **Transparency Reports**: Quarterly disclosure of government requests, takedowns, and compliance actions.
- **No Deceptive Marketing**: All claims about security, privacy, and anonymity must be technically accurate and independently verifiable.
- **Community Governance**: Protocol changes require open deliberation and rough consensus. No unilateral modifications to the Sovereign Core.
- **Localization Accessibility**: Provide a language dropdown with translations for the most important languages. English is the primary language. On first visit, auto-select based on browser language or IP-derived region, with a clear manual override and a persistent user preference. Supported languages: English, Spanish, Portuguese, Italian, French, German, Ukrainian, Russian, Polish, Hungarian, Korean, Japanese, Chinese, Persian/Farsi (Iran), and Arabic (Arab countries).

---

## What Railgun Is Not

- Not a tool for harassment, doxxing, or stalking
- Not a platform for coordinating illegal violence
- Not a weapon against any person, institution, or government
- Not a promise of immunity from consequences
- Not an ideology—it is infrastructure

---

## The Measure of Success

Railgun succeeds when:
- A journalist can publish evidence without fear
- A family can communicate across hostile borders
- A community can organize mutual aid without surveillance
- A whistleblower can expose wrongdoing safely
- A society can correct itself without bloodshed

Railgun fails when:
- Its tools are used to harm innocents
- Its rhetoric inspires violence
- Its operators betray user trust
- Its protocol becomes dependent on any single entity
